Abstract

We map the PAH3.3 μm emission in 672 galaxies (453 cluster, 219 field) at z ~ 0.2-0.4, using JWST NIRCam medium-band photometry across six deep fields: Abell 2744, MACS0416, Abell 370, COSMOS, UDS, and GOODS-South. Together with ancillary HST data, each galaxy is detected in 15-30+ filters from UV to 5 μm.

We find that only 7.5% of cluster galaxies are PAH emitters, compared to 44.7% in the field — a factor of ~6 suppression. The fraction of PAH emitters drops sharply in the UVJ transition zone within clusters, even at stellar masses and star formation rates comparable to field analogues. This cannot be explained solely by different SFRs, pointing to direct environmental destruction or removal of PAH molecules by ram pressure stripping (RPS) and related processes.

PAH Extraction Methodology

PAH flux formula

For each galaxy at redshift z, we identify the central filter FC containing PAH3.3 and two flanking filters FL, FR. The PAH flux is:

FPAH = wFC [ fFC - (1-k)fFL - k·fFR ]

Continuum estimated by linear interpolation. Computed pixel-by-pixel with SNR > 5 and EW > 200 Å cuts.

Galaxy classification

PAH emitters defined by sPAH ≥ 10-26.5 erg s-1cm-2M⊙-1 to isolate ISM physics from galaxy mass scale. UVJ diagram used to separate active, transition, and passive populations.

Companion Study: PAH in Ram-Pressure Stripped Galaxies at z ~ 0.3  (Benotto et al. 2026b)

In a companion paper we analyse 9 RPS jellyfish galaxies in Abell 2744. PAH3.3 emission is detected in stripped tail clumps outside the galactic disk, with asymmetric PAH maps aligned with the stripping direction.

Fireball model: age gradients along the tails — younger clumps further from the disk — are observed for the first time beyond the Local Universe, consistent with gas collapse timescales after stripping.

  • PAH-SFR correlation holds in stripped clumps for ages < 1 Gyr
  • PAH in clumps must either form in <10 Myr (excluding AGB origin) or be directly stripped with the atomic gas
  • RPS may clear PAH molecules rapidly, explaining the low transition-zone fraction in the statistical sample

Discussion

SFR cannot explain PAH deficit alone

In the UVJ transition zone, cluster galaxies have SFRSED = 0.08 ± 0.04 M⊙/yr vs 0.17 ± 0.05 M⊙/yr in the field — a factor of ~2. Yet the PAH fraction differs by a factor of ~5-10. The SFH shapes are statistically identical. This decoupling indicates that PAH molecules are being destroyed or removed independently of ongoing star formation.

Environmental mechanisms

  • Lower median AV in cluster transition galaxies (0.1 vs 0.3 mag) confirms dust and PAH removal
  • RPS can strip PAH molecules along with atomic gas on short timescales
  • Strangulation / starvation truncates gas supply, halting PAH replenishment
  • Effect persists to R200, suggesting pre-processing or long orbital timescales

PAH as environmental tracer

PAH3.3 emission responds to cluster environment earlier and more sensitively than SED-based SFR, making it a powerful diagnostic of ISM conditions during galaxy transformation.

Conclusions
  • Only 7.5% of cluster galaxies are PAH emitters at z ~ 0.2-0.4, vs 44.7% in the field — a factor of ~6 environmental suppression.
  • Suppression is strongest in the UVJ transition zone, with cluster fractions <10% at log M★ < 10.2 M⊙ up to R200.
  • The deficit cannot be explained by SFR differences alone — environmental processes physically remove or destroy PAH molecules.
  • Lower dust extinction (AV = 0.1 vs 0.3 mag) in cluster transition galaxies confirms dust + PAH stripping by ram pressure.
  • In RPS tail clumps (Benotto+2026b), PAH emission traces the fireball model — first detection beyond the Local Universe.
  • PAH3.3 is a sensitive, independent tracer of cluster environmental effects on the ISM, complementary to SED-based SFR.
