  • B. Vulcani, P. Watson, et al.

PAH Emission and Age Properties of Ram Pressure Stripped Galaxies at z~0.3
  • Detection of age gradients in the ram pressure stripped tails of galaxies at z~0.3, consistent with the Fireball model — observed for the first time beyond the local Universe.
  • Strong PAH emission detected in star-forming clumps of ram pressure stripped galaxies, suggesting that PAH molecules may be stripped along with the atomic gas.

  • G. Rodighiero, L. Bisigello, et al.

Ultra High-Redshift or Closer-by, Dust-Obscured Galaxies? Deciphering the Nature of Faint, Previously Missed F200W-Dropouts in CEERS
  • Detection of five faint objects that are either extremely dusty HELM (i.e., dwarfs characterized by a puzzlingly high dust content) galaxies or ultra high redshift objects between 15 < z < 20, whose stellar masses are not in tension with ΛCDM cosmology.
  • Discovery of three z < 4 HELM galaxies which occupy the high-mass end of the stellar mass distribution of HELM objects in CEERS
  • Development of an analysis pipeline that combines different SED-fitting codes, templates, SFHs, dust extinction models and stacking which can be helpful in future UHR object searches

Investigating the Star Formation History of Galaxies in the First Billion Years of the Universe
  • SED fitting of spectroscopic JWST data of the z>6 galaxies in the JADES survey.
  • Detection of mass and redshift dependence in the specific star formation rate of galaxies.
  • In-depth analysis of the Bagpipes SED fitting code applied to spectroscopic data, including critical evaluation, custom modifications, and assessment of its performance at high redshift.
  • Study of the JWST NIRSpec/prism spectroscopic resolution and its impact on the SED fitting of ultra-high-redshift galaxy spectra.
